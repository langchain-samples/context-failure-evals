## About Materialize

Materialize is a fast-growing data infrastructure company that provides real-time streaming SQL databases for modern applications. Founded in 2017 by a team of database veterans, the company has quickly established itself as a key player in the streaming data space, competing with established players like Confluent and emerging startups in the real-time analytics market.

The company's flagship product allows developers to build applications that can query streaming data using standard SQL, eliminating the complexity traditionally associated with stream processing. With offices in New York, Austin, and London, Materialize serves customers ranging from high-growth startups to Fortune 500 enterprises across fintech, e-commerce, and IoT sectors.

Under CEO Frank Morrison's leadership, Materialize has grown from a small team of 15 engineers to over 400 employees globally. The company completed its Series C funding round in late 2022, raising significant capital to expand its engineering team and accelerate product development. The remote-first culture, established during the pandemic, continues to be a core part of the company's identity, though they maintain collaborative spaces in major tech hubs.

## Company Culture & Values

**Innovation-First Mindset:** Materialize prides itself on solving complex technical challenges that other companies won't tackle. The engineering culture is heavily influenced by academic research, with several team members holding PhDs in distributed systems and database theory.

**Transparency & Open Communication:** Regular all-hands meetings, open Slack channels, and accessible leadership team. Employees frequently cite the ability to directly message executives and get thoughtful responses.

**Work-Life Balance:** Strong emphasis on sustainable work practices. Unlimited PTO policy is reportedly well-used, with management actively encouraging time off. Flexible hours accommodate different time zones across their global team.

**Diversity & Inclusion:** Active D&I initiatives including employee resource groups, bias training for hiring managers, and partnerships with organizations like Code2040 and Girls Who Code.
