# Leadership Team

## Meet the Leaders Driving Materialize Forward

Our leadership team combines decades of experience in distributed systems, databases, and enterprise software. Together, they're building the future of real-time data infrastructure.

### Nate Stewart, CEO & Co-Founder

Nate Stewart founded Materialize in 2019 with a vision to make streaming data analytics accessible to every developer. With over 12 years of experience in distributed systems and data infrastructure, Nate previously served as Principal Engineer at Confluent, where he led the development of ksqlDB's query optimization engine.

Before Confluent, Nate spent five years at LinkedIn working on real-time data pipelines that processed billions of events daily. He holds a Master's degree in Computer Science from Stanford University and a Bachelor's degree from MIT.

*"The gap between the importance of real-time data and the difficulty of working with it has only grown wider. At Materialize, we're not just building another database – we're reimagining how developers interact with streaming data. Our goal is to make real-time analytics as natural as writing a SQL query, because that's exactly what it should be."*

Nate is a frequent speaker at data conferences and has published research on differential dataflow and streaming query optimization. When he's not thinking about databases, he enjoys rock climbing and exploring New York's food scene.

### Dr. Sarah Martinez, VP of Engineering

Dr. Sarah Martinez brings over 15 years of engineering leadership experience to Materialize, with a particular focus on building scalable distributed systems. Before joining Materialize in 2020, Sarah was a Senior Staff Engineer at Google, where she led the team responsible for Spanner's query processing engine.

Sarah holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from UC Berkeley, where her dissertation focused on consensus algorithms in distributed databases. She has authored over 20 papers on distributed systems and database architecture, and holds 15 patents in the field.

*"What excites me most about Materialize is the technical depth required to solve these problems correctly. We're not just building fast software – we're building correct software that happens to be fast. The combination of strong consistency guarantees with real-time performance requires innovations at every layer of the stack."*

Sarah is passionate about building inclusive engineering teams and serves on the advisory board for Women in Databases, a nonprofit organization supporting women in data infrastructure careers.

### Michael Chen, VP of Sales

Michael Chen joined Materialize in 2021 to lead the company's go-to-market efforts. With over 10 years of enterprise software sales experience, Michael previously served as Regional Sales Director at Snowflake, where he helped grow the company's revenue from $50M to $500M ARR.

Before Snowflake, Michael spent six years at Palantir, starting as a Solutions Engineer and eventually leading the Financial Services vertical. He holds an MBA from Wharton and a Bachelor's degree in Engineering from Carnegie Mellon.

*"The beauty of selling Materialize is that we're solving real problems that keep CTOs awake at night. When you can show a customer how to eliminate hours of latency from their decision-making process using familiar SQL, the value proposition sells itself. We're not asking customers to rebuild their entire stack – we're making their existing stack dramatically more powerful."*

Michael is an active mentor in the enterprise software community and regularly speaks at sales leadership conferences.

### Dr. Rachel Thompson, VP of Product

Dr. Rachel Thompson leads product strategy and development at Materialize, focusing on making complex streaming systems accessible to mainstream developers. Rachel joined in early 2022 from Databricks, where she was Principal Product Manager for Delta Lake and streaming analytics features.

Prior to Databricks, Rachel spent seven years at Microsoft working on Azure SQL Database and Cosmos DB. She holds a Ph.D. in Database Systems from Carnegie Mellon University and has published extensively on streaming analytics and developer experience.

*"The challenge with streaming databases isn't just making them fast or correct – it's making them intuitive. Developers shouldn't need to learn a new mental model to work with real-time data. Our job is to hide the complexity of streaming systems behind interfaces that feel familiar and natural."*

Rachel is a strong advocate for developer-centric product design and frequently writes about the intersection of database technology and user experience.

### James Wu, VP of Marketing

James Wu joined Materialize in 2022 to lead marketing and developer relations. With a background spanning both technical and marketing roles, James previously served as Director of Developer Marketing at HashiCorp, where he built the company's developer community from 10,000 to over 500,000 active users.

Before HashiCorp, James was a software engineer at several startups and holds a degree in Computer Science from UC Berkeley. His unique combination of technical depth and marketing expertise helps bridge the gap between complex technology and clear communication.

*"Materialize represents a fundamental shift in how we think about data infrastructure, but that shift only matters if developers understand and adopt it. My role is to help the broader community understand not just what we've built, but why it matters and how it can transform their applications."*

James is a regular contributor to technical publications and maintains an active presence in the streaming data community.

### Lisa Rodriguez, Chief Financial Officer

Lisa Rodriguez joined Materialize as CFO in 2023, bringing over 12 years of financial leadership experience in high-growth technology companies. Most recently, she served as VP of Finance at Cockroach Labs, where she helped scale the company through multiple funding rounds and international expansion.

Prior to CockroachDB, Lisa was Director of Finance at MongoDB during the company's IPO and subsequent public company growth. She holds an MBA from Columbia Business School and is a CPA.

*"Materialize is at an inflection point where product-market fit is driving rapid growth, and my job is to ensure we have the financial foundation to scale efficiently. The market opportunity for real-time analytics is enormous, and we're building the systems and processes to capture it responsibly."*

Lisa serves on the board of CFOs for Growth, an organization supporting finance leaders at scaling technology companies.
