# About Materialize

## Transforming Real-Time Data Infrastructure

At Materialize, we're building the future of real-time data analytics. Our mission is to make streaming data as easy to work with as traditional batch data, empowering organizations to make decisions at the speed of thought rather than the speed of overnight batch jobs.

Founded in 2019 by CEO Nate Stewart and a team of passionate data engineers, Materialize has grown from a small startup with a big vision into a thriving company headquartered in New York City.

### Our Product

Materialize is a cloud-native streaming SQL database that delivers fresh, consistent results with ultra-low latency. Unlike traditional approaches that force you to choose between speed and consistency, Materialize gives you both. Our platform allows you to:

- Query constantly changing data with standard SQL
- Maintain complex views over streaming data in real-time
- Scale from prototype to production without rewriting queries
- Integrate seamlessly with your existing data stack

Built on a foundation of differential dataflow and powered by Rust, Materialize processes millions of events per second while maintaining ACID consistency guarantees. We're not just another streaming platform – we're the first SQL database designed from the ground up for the streaming era.

In a world where data freshness can mean the difference between profit and loss, Materialize occupies a unique position in the real-time analytics space. ### Our Founding Story

The idea for Materialize was born from frustration. Nate Stewart, having spent years working with streaming data at scale, recognized a fundamental problem: while the world was generating more real-time data than ever before, the tools to analyze it remained fragmented, complex, and inaccessible to most developers.

"I was tired of seeing brilliant engineers spending months building custom streaming infrastructure instead of solving their actual business problems," Stewart recalls. "There had to be a better way – one that made streaming data as approachable as querying a traditional database."

Starting with a small team in a cramped WeWork space in Manhattan, Stewart and the founding team began building what would become Materialize. The vision was ambitious: create a system that could handle the complexity of streaming data processing while exposing a simple, familiar SQL interface.

The early days weren't easy. Streaming databases represent a fundamentally different approach to data processing, requiring innovations in everything from storage engines to query optimization. But the team's persistence paid off when early customers began reporting dramatic improvements in their ability to act on real-time insights.
