# Building a Culture of Excellence: Our Growth Story

*Posted on July 12, 2023*

This month, we reached an exciting milestone at Materialize. As I reflect on this journey from a small team of founders to the company we are today, I'm struck by how intentionally we've had to think about culture, values, and what it means to build something meaningful together.

When Nate Stewart and I started Materialize in 2019, we knew that the technical challenges ahead would require not just brilliant individual contributors, but a team that could collaborate effectively, debate respectfully, and maintain high standards as we scaled. The problems we're solving – building a streaming SQL database from the ground up – demand the kind of sustained technical excellence that only comes from great culture.

### The Early Days

I still remember our first office – a cramped space in a shared building in SoMa, with folding tables from IKEA and ethernet cables running along the walls. Our entire engineering team could fit around a single table for lunch, and we all knew each other's code review preferences by heart.

But even in those early days, certain cultural elements were evident. We had a bias toward written communication and thorough documentation. We insisted on code reviews that focused on clarity and maintainability, not just correctness. And we established a practice of "demo Fridays" where anyone could show off what they'd been working on, from major features to small improvements to entirely experimental projects.

"The foundation we built in those first months has been crucial," reflects Alex Chen, our CTO who helped establish many of our early engineering practices. "We weren't just building software – we were building the processes and cultural norms that would allow us to keep building excellent software as we grew."

### Defining Our Values

As we began hiring beyond the founding team, we realized we needed to be more explicit about what we valued and how we worked together. After months of discussion and iteration, we landed on five core values that continue to guide our decision-making:

**Technical Excellence**: We believe that great software is built through attention to detail, rigorous testing, and constant learning. We're not afraid to spend extra time getting something right, and we celebrate elegant solutions to hard problems.

**Intellectual Honesty**: We admit when we don't know something, acknowledge when we make mistakes, and change our minds when presented with better information. Ego has no place in building great technology.

**Customer Obsession**: Every decision we make starts with understanding our users' needs. We regularly talk to customers, analyze usage patterns, and prioritize features that deliver real value.

**Inclusive Excellence**: We believe diverse teams build better products. We actively work to create an environment where everyone can do their best work, regardless of background or experience level.

**Long-Term Thinking**: We're building infrastructure that companies will depend on for years to come. We make architectural decisions with longevity in mind and invest in foundational work that pays dividends over time.

### Employee Spotlights: Living Our Values

The best way to understand our culture is through the people who embody it every day. Here are a few stories from team members who exemplify what makes Materialize special:

**Dr. Lisa Park, Senior Research Engineer**

Lisa joined us from Google Brain in 2021, drawn by the opportunity to apply cutting-edge research to real-world streaming data problems. She's been instrumental in developing our query optimization algorithms and has published three papers on streaming analytics during her time here.

"What I love about Materialize is the balance between rigorous engineering and innovative research," Lisa explains. "We're not just implementing known solutions – we're pushing the boundaries of what's possible with streaming databases. But we're doing it with the discipline and testing practices you'd expect from production infrastructure."

Lisa also leads our mentorship program, pairing senior engineers with new hires to help them ramp up on our complex codebase. "Technical excellence isn't just about writing good code," she notes. "It's about sharing knowledge and helping others grow."

**Marcus Rodriguez, Director of Customer Success**

Marcus came to us from Snowflake in 2022, bringing extensive experience helping enterprise customers adopt new data technologies. He's built our customer success organization from scratch and maintains relationships with our largest accounts.

"Customer obsession means different things at different companies," Marcus observes. "At Materialize, it means really understanding the technical challenges our users face and working backward from there. I spend probably 30% of my time in Slack channels and support tickets, not because I have to, but because direct customer feedback makes me better at my job."

Under Marcus's leadership, our customer satisfaction scores have consistently exceeded 95%, and our net revenue retention has grown to over 150%.

**Jennifer Wu, Staff Software Engineer**

Jennifer was one of our earliest employees, joining straight from her Ph.D. program at MIT. She's grown with the company from individual contributor to tech lead to staff engineer, and she's been central to building our distributed storage layer.

"The thing I appreciate most about our culture is how we balance high standards with psychological safety," Jennifer reflects. "We have incredibly rigorous code review and design review processes, but they're focused on making the work better, not making people feel bad. I've learned more in three years here than I did in six years of graduate school."

Jennifer also co-leads our diversity and inclusion initiatives, helping us recruit from a broader range of schools and backgrounds. "Inclusive excellence isn't just the right thing to do – it makes us better engineers," she explains.

### Remote Work and Office Culture

Like many companies, we had to rapidly adapt to remote work in 2020. But unlike some companies that saw remote work as a temporary necessity, we embraced it as an opportunity to build a more inclusive and flexible culture.

Today, about 60% of our team works primarily remotely, while 40% comes into our New York office regularly. We've invested heavily in making both experiences excellent – our office features state-of-the-art collaboration spaces and AV equipment, while our remote tools and processes ensure that distributed team members never feel like second-class citizens.

"The key is being intentional about inclusion," explains Sarah Kim, our VP of People Operations. "We design every meeting, every decision-making process, and every social event with both in-person and remote participants in mind. It requires more planning, but it results in better outcomes for everyone."

Our "hybrid-first" approach has also helped us recruit from a much broader talent pool. Some of our best engineers are based in cities where we don't have offices, and they bring perspectives and experiences that make our team stronger.

### Challenges and Growth

Building culture at a fast-growing startup isn't without challenges. We've had to evolve our practices as we've scaled, and we've made mistakes along the way.

One of our biggest challenges has been maintaining the close-knit feel of a small team as we've grown. We've addressed this through smaller team structures, cross-functional projects, and regular all-hands meetings where everyone can share what they're working on.

We've also had to be more systematic about onboarding, professional development, and career progression. What worked when we could individually mentor every new hire doesn't scale to multiple hires per week.

"Scaling a team rapidly requires different organizational muscles," notes CEO Nate Stewart. "We've had to become much more intentional about communication, goal-setting, and making sure everyone understands how their work contributes to our mission."

### Looking Forward

As we continue to grow – we're hiring across all teams – we're focused on preserving what makes Materialize special while adapting to the realities of a larger organization.

We're investing in better career development frameworks, more sophisticated project management processes, and enhanced benefits and perks. But at the core, we remain committed to the values and practices that have gotten us this far: technical excellence, intellectual honesty, customer obsession, inclusive excellence, and long-term thinking.

The streaming database space is evolving rapidly, and the challenges ahead will require all of our collective intelligence and creativity. But with the team and culture we've built, I'm confident that our best days are ahead of us.

If you're interested in joining us – whether as an engineer, product manager, salesperson, or in any other role – I encourage you to check out our careers page. We're looking for people who share our values and want to help build the future of real-time data infrastructure.

Here's to building technology that makes a difference in the world.
