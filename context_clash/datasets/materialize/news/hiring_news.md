# Materialize Goes on Massive Hiring Spree, Plans to Triple Workforce Across Five Cities

**The streaming database unicorn aims to add 800+ employees by mid-2024 as enterprise demand surges for real-time data platforms**

Materialize, the fast-growing streaming database company valued at over $1.2 billion and backed by over $100 million in total venture funding, announced today an aggressive expansion plan that will see the company nearly triple its workforce from 240 to over 800 employees by the middle of 2024. The hiring surge spans five major metropolitan areas and represents one of the largest talent acquisition efforts in the enterprise software sector this year.

The expansion comes as Materialize experiences explosive growth in enterprise adoption of its real-time analytics platform. The company, which processes over 10 billion events daily for customers ranging from Fortune 500 financial institutions to high-growth technology companies, has seen revenue grow over 500% year-over-year as organizations increasingly demand immediate insights from their streaming data.

"We're at an inflection point where real-time data has moved from nice-to-have to mission-critical," said Rachel Martinez, Materialize's newly appointed Chief People Officer, who joined from Snowflake earlier this year. "Our customers are building applications that simply can't function with stale data, and that's driving unprecedented demand for our platform. We need to scale our team to match that market opportunity."

The hiring initiative will focus on five key metropolitan areas: New York City, San Francisco, Austin, Boston, and Chicago. This geographic expansion represents a significant shift for the company, which has historically concentrated its workforce in its New York headquarters and a smaller San Francisco office.

The decision to establish significant presences in Austin, Boston, and Chicago reflects both talent market dynamics and customer proximity considerations. Austin's thriving tech ecosystem and favorable business environment have attracted numerous enterprise software companies, while Boston offers access to world-class university talent and established financial services customers. Chicago provides a central location for serving Midwest enterprise customers and offers competitive talent acquisition costs.

"We're not just hiring for the sake of hiring," emphasized Martinez. "Each of these locations offers distinct advantages. Austin gives us access to incredible engineering talent from companies like Dell, IBM, and the growing startup ecosystem. Boston connects us to top-tier universities and the financial services hub. Chicago offers operational advantages and puts us closer to manufacturing and retail customers."

The breakdown of planned hires by function reflects Materialize's priorities as it scales:

**Engineering (450 new hires)**: The majority of new positions will focus on core platform development, with particular emphasis on distributed systems engineers, database specialists, and cloud infrastructure experts. The company is also creating entirely new teams focused on developer experience, security, and enterprise integration capabilities.

**Sales and Customer Success (180 new hires)**: As Materialize expands into enterprise accounts, the company is building out dedicated sales teams in each geographic region, supported by technical specialists and customer success managers. Plans include hiring seasoned enterprise sales professionals from companies like Oracle, SAP, and Palantir.

**Marketing and Developer Relations (85 new hires)**: The company aims to significantly expand its developer community and enterprise brand presence, with new roles spanning content marketing, developer advocacy, and demand generation.

**Operations and Support (85 new hires)**: To support 24/7 enterprise customers and global expansion, Materialize is building out comprehensive support and operations teams, including roles in customer support, site reliability engineering, and business operations.

The hiring surge is supported by Materialize's recent $185 million Series D funding round, led by General Catalyst with participation from existing investors including Kleiner Perkins and Lightspeed Venture Partners. The funding provides the company with substantial runway to execute its expansion plans while continuing to invest heavily in product development.

"This is exactly the kind of aggressive scaling we want to see from our portfolio companies," noted Sarah Chen, partner at General Catalyst who led the investment. "Materialize has achieved clear product-market fit, and the market opportunity for real-time data infrastructure is enormous. The right strategy is to scale quickly while they have competitive advantage and market momentum."

The company's compensation packages reflect the competitive talent market, with total compensation reportedly ranging from $180,000 to $350,000 for senior engineering roles, including significant equity components. For leadership positions, packages can exceed $500,000 annually, positioning Materialize competitively with other high-growth enterprise software companies.

"We're competing for talent with every major tech company and well-funded startup," acknowledged Martinez. "Our advantage is that we're solving genuinely interesting technical problems at massive scale, we have a clear path to becoming a category-defining company, and we can offer meaningful equity upside. That combination is attractive to the caliber of people we want to hire."

The geographic expansion also reflects changing work patterns in the post-pandemic era. While Materialize will maintain significant office presences in each location, the company is embracing a hybrid work model that allows employees flexibility in how and where they work. This approach is designed to attract top talent regardless of their location preferences.

"The best engineers and sales professionals have options," noted Martinez. "We need to meet them where they are and offer the kind of flexibility that enables peak performance. That means investing in great office spaces while also supporting remote and hybrid work arrangements."

The expansion timeline is ambitious, with Materialize aiming to complete approximately 60% of the hiring by the end of Q1 2024. The company has already begun interviewing for senior leadership roles in each new market, including general managers who will be responsible for local team building and customer relationships.

To support the hiring surge, Materialize has partnered with executive search firm Heidrick & Struggles for senior roles while building an internal recruiting team of over 25 professionals. The company is also investing in employer branding initiatives, including participation in major industry conferences, university recruiting programs, and developer community events.

"Scale is critical in enterprise software," explained industry analyst Michael Park from Bessemer Venture Partners. "Companies that can execute rapid but thoughtful expansion often capture disproportionate market share. Materialize appears to be making the right investments at the right time, assuming they can maintain quality while scaling this quickly."

The hiring expansion positions Materialize to compete more effectively with established players like Snowflake and Databricks, both of which have significantly larger workforces and global presences. It also demonstrates confidence in the long-term market opportunity for real-time data infrastructure, even as broader venture capital markets have become more conservative.

For job seekers interested in joining Materialize's expansion, the company has launched a dedicated careers portal (materialize.com/careers) with detailed information about available positions, office locations, and application processes. The company is hosting virtual information sessions and in-person meetups in each target market to engage potential candidates.

"We're building something genuinely transformative," concluded Martinez. "The companies that win in the next decade will be those that can act on real-time information faster than their competitors. We're providing the data infrastructure that makes that possible, and we need the best people in the industry to help us execute that vision."
