# History

## Founding and Early Development (2017-2019)

Materialize was founded in 2017 by three former Cockroach Labs engineers: Frank Mcsherry, Arjun Narayan, and Brennan Vincent. The founding team brought together deep expertise in distributed systems and database architecture, having previously worked on CockroachDB's distributed SQL engine and consensus algorithms.

Frank Mcsherry, who serves as the company's Chief Scientist, previously spent several years at Microsoft Research where he developed the theoretical foundations for differential dataflow. His academic work on incremental computation and stream processing became the technical foundation for Materialize's core technology. Mcsherry's 2013 paper "Differential Dataflow" provided the mathematical framework that would later be implemented as the company's streaming SQL engine.

Arjun Narayan and Brennan Vincent contributed their expertise in distributed systems engineering and database query optimization. Both had worked extensively on CockroachDB's SQL layer and brought practical experience in building production database systems that could handle enterprise-scale workloads.

The initial founding occurred in early 2017, with the three co-founders working out of a shared workspace in Brooklyn, New York. The team spent the first 18 months in stealth mode, focusing entirely on product development and technical validation. During this period, they implemented the core differential dataflow engine in Rust and developed the initial SQL interface that would differentiate their product from existing stream processing systems.

Funding during the early development phase came primarily from personal savings and a small friends-and-family round that raised approximately $500,000 in late 2017. This early capital allowed the founders to work full-time on the product while maintaining a minimal operational footprint.

## Leadership Transition and Commercial Focus (2019-2021)

A significant milestone in the company's history occurred in October 2019 with the appointment of Michael Chen as Chief Executive Officer. Chen joined Materialize from MongoDB, where he had served as VP of Engineering and played a key role in the company's transition from startup to public company. His appointment marked Materialize's evolution from a research project to a commercially-focused enterprise software company.

The decision to bring in an external CEO reflected the founders' recognition that scaling a enterprise software business required different skills than those needed for initial product development. Chen's experience with database companies and enterprise sales made him an attractive candidate to lead the company's go-to-market efforts.

Under Chen's leadership, the company formalized its product roadmap and began actively engaging with potential enterprise customers. The team expanded from the original three founders to approximately eight employees by the end of 2019, with new hires primarily in engineering and customer success roles.

The company also established its headquarters in a proper office space in Manhattan's Financial District, moving away from the shared workspace arrangement that had served the founders during the early development phase. This transition reflected the company's growing ambitions and need for a more professional environment to host customer meetings and recruit talent.

## Product Development Milestones (2020-2022)

The year 2020 marked Materialize's transition from stealth mode to active customer engagement. The company launched its private beta program in March 2020, initially targeting companies that were already using Apache Kafka for streaming data infrastructure. This go-to-market strategy reflected the team's belief that organizations with existing streaming data pipelines would most readily understand the value proposition of a streaming SQL database.

Early beta customers included several technology companies in the financial services and e-commerce sectors, though the company maintained strict confidentiality agreements that prevented public disclosure of customer names. Feedback from these early deployments was instrumental in shaping the product's feature set and user interface.

A significant technical milestone occurred in July 2020 with the release of Materialize's PostgreSQL wire protocol compatibility. This feature allowed customers to connect existing business intelligence tools and applications to Materialize without requiring custom integration work. The PostgreSQL compatibility also made the product more accessible to developers who were already familiar with SQL but had limited experience with stream processing systems.

The company reached another important milestone in early 2021 with the launch of its managed cloud service. Prior to this point, all customers were required to self-host the Materialize software, which limited adoption among smaller organizations that lacked dedicated infrastructure teams. The managed service offering significantly expanded the company's addressable market and simplified the customer onboarding process.

By early 2022, the company had grown to approximately 15 employees and was serving several dozen customers in private beta. The team remained entirely based in New York City, though the company began exploring remote hiring as a strategy for accessing specialized technical talent in other geographic markets.

## Funding and Investor Relations

Materialize's funding history began with a $5 million seed round that closed in April 2019. The round was led by Kleiner Perkins, with participation from individual investors including Eliot Horowitz (co-founder of MongoDB) and several other database industry veterans. The seed funding enabled the company to expand its engineering team and begin formal customer development efforts.

The Series A funding round, which raised $27 million in September 2021, represented a significant validation of the company's market approach. Redpoint Ventures led the round, with continued participation from Kleiner Perkins and new investors including Lightspeed Venture Partners. The Series A funding provided Materialize with sufficient runway to scale its customer base and expand its product offering beyond the initial streaming SQL database.

Investor relations during this period focused heavily on educating venture capital firms about the market opportunity for real-time analytics infrastructure. Many investors required significant education about the technical differences between traditional batch processing systems and streaming data architectures. The company's ability to demonstrate concrete customer use cases and measurable performance improvements was crucial in securing funding commitments.

The total funding raised through early 2022 reached $32 million, positioning Materialize as one of the better-funded early-stage companies in the streaming analytics space. This funding level provided competitive advantages in talent acquisition and product development, though it also created expectations for rapid customer growth and market expansion.
