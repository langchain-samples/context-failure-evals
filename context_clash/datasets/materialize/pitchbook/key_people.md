# Materialize Inc. - Key Personnel

## Executive Leadership Team

### Nate Stewart - Chief Executive Officer & Co-Founder

Nate Stewart co-founded Materialize in 2018 and serves as CEO, bringing over 15 years of experience in distributed systems and data infrastructure. Prior to Materialize, Stewart was a Senior Principal Engineer at Cockroach Labs, where he led the development of distributed SQL capabilities and helped scale the platform through its Series B growth phase.

Before Cockroach Labs, Stewart spent six years at Google, working on critical infrastructure projects including Spanner and BigQuery. His experience with large-scale distributed systems architecture provided foundational insights that shaped Materialize's approach to real-time data processing. Stewart holds a PhD in Computer Science from MIT, where his dissertation focused on consistency models in distributed databases.

As CEO, Stewart has successfully led Materialize through four funding rounds totaling $167.2M, building the company from a technical prototype to a commercial platform serving over 200 enterprise customers. His technical background enables hands-on involvement in product development while scaling the organization's go-to-market capabilities.

### Marcus Chen - Chief Technology Officer

Marcus Chen joined Materialize as CTO in early 2020, bringing deep expertise in streaming systems and real-time analytics from his previous role as Staff Engineer at Confluent. At Confluent, Chen was instrumental in developing KSQL and Kafka Streams capabilities, contributing to the company's expansion beyond traditional message queuing into stream processing applications.

Prior to Confluent, Chen spent five years at LinkedIn, where he worked on the original Kafka development team and contributed to the platform's evolution from an internal messaging system to the foundational streaming infrastructure used across the technology industry. His expertise in stream processing semantics and distributed systems reliability has been crucial to Materialize's technical differentiation.

Chen holds a Master's degree in Computer Science from Stanford University and has published extensively on stream processing architectures and consistency models. Under his technical leadership, Materialize has developed proprietary incremental view maintenance algorithms that provide significant performance advantages over traditional stream processing approaches.

### Sarah Mitchell - VP of Engineering

Sarah Mitchell oversees Materialize's engineering organization, managing a team of 85+ engineers across platform development, cloud infrastructure, and customer integration teams. Mitchell joined Materialize in 2021 from Snowflake, where she served as Senior Director of Engineering for the company's real-time features and streaming capabilities.

Her tenure at Snowflake included leading the development of Snowpipe and Tasks functionality, giving her deep insights into customer requirements for real-time data processing workflows. Prior to Snowflake, Mitchell held senior engineering roles at Palantir and Oracle, focusing on large-scale data processing and analytics platforms.

Mitchell's leadership has been instrumental in scaling Materialize's engineering culture while maintaining high code quality and system reliability standards. She holds a BS in Computer Engineering from UC Berkeley and has been recognized as one of the "Top 40 Under 40" technology leaders by Silicon Valley Business Journal.

### David Park - Chief Revenue Officer

David Park joined Materialize as CRO in 2023, bringing extensive experience in enterprise software sales from his previous role as VP of Sales at Databricks. At Databricks, Park built and led the company's enterprise sales organization during a period of rapid growth, helping scale ARR from $50M to over $200M.

Prior to Databricks, Park spent eight years at Salesforce in progressively senior sales leadership roles, including Regional VP for the West Coast Enterprise division. His experience selling complex technical solutions to enterprise customers has been valuable in positioning Materialize's real-time analytics platform with Fortune 500 accounts.

Park's go-to-market strategy has focused on expanding Materialize's presence in financial services and e-commerce verticals, where real-time data processing requirements align closely with the platform's capabilities. Under his leadership, average contract values have increased 180% year-over-year.

## Board of Directors

### Martin Casado - Board Member (Andreessen Horowitz)

Martin Casado represents Andreessen Horowitz on Materialize's board following a16z's Series C lead investment. As a General Partner focusing on enterprise infrastructure investments, Casado brings extensive experience in networking and distributed systems technologies.

Casado is best known as the co-founder of Nicira, which was acquired by VMware for $1.26B in 2012. His work on software-defined networking has been foundational to modern cloud infrastructure architectures. At a16z, Casado has led investments in companies including Databricks, PagerDuty, and Optimizely.

His board contributions focus on technical product strategy and enterprise customer development, leveraging his experience building and scaling infrastructure software companies.

### Mamoon Hamid - Board Member (Kleiner Perkins)

Mamoon Hamid joined Materialize's board in connection with Kleiner Perkins' Series B leadership. As a Partner at KP, Hamid focuses on enterprise software and developer tools investments, with a portfolio including Slack, Figma, and LaunchDarkly.

Prior to joining Kleiner Perkins, Hamid was a Partner at Social Capital, where he led investments in enterprise software companies. His operational experience includes senior roles at Zynga during its rapid scaling phase, providing valuable insights into managing high-growth technology organizations.

Hamid's board involvement emphasizes go-to-market strategy, competitive positioning, and organizational scaling challenges as Materialize transitions from startup to growth-stage company.

### Guru Chahal - Board Member (Lightspeed Venture Partners)

Guru Chahal represents Lightspeed Venture Partners following their Series A lead investment. As a Partner at Lightspeed, Chahal focuses on enterprise infrastructure and developer tools, with investments including Affirm, Carta, and Nutanix.

Chahal's background includes operational roles in enterprise software, having served as VP of Business Development at VMware during its growth from startup to public company. His experience spans both technical product development and enterprise sales execution.

His board contributions focus on strategic partnerships, customer development, and market expansion opportunities, particularly in international markets where Lightspeed has strong portfolio company networks.

### Sarah Chen - Independent Director

Sarah Chen serves as an independent board member, bringing operational expertise from her previous role as VP of Product at Snowflake. During her tenure at Snowflake, Chen led product development for the company's real-time and streaming capabilities, providing directly relevant experience for Materialize's market positioning.

Prior to Snowflake, Chen held senior product roles at Oracle and IBM, focusing on database and analytics products. Her technical background includes a PhD in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University, with research focus on distributed systems and database architecture.

Chen's independent perspective provides valuable guidance on product strategy, competitive differentiation, and technology market dynamics as Materialize continues scaling its platform capabilities.
