# Materialize Inc. - Funding History

## Overview
Materialize has raised $167.2M across four funding rounds since its founding in 2018, demonstrating consistent investor confidence in the company's real-time data infrastructure platform. The company's valuation has grown from $45M at Series A to $850M at its most recent Series C, reflecting strong market traction and revenue growth in the competitive streaming analytics space.

---

## Series C - April 2024
**Amount:** $60.0M  
**Pre-Money Valuation:** $790M  
**Post-Money Valuation:** $850M  
**Lead Investor:** Andreessen Horowitz (a16z)  
**Participants:** Kleiner Perkins, Redpoint Ventures, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Bessemer Venture Partners

Materialize's Series C round was anchored by Andreessen Horowitz's Infrastructure team, marking their first investment in the company. The funding was raised to accelerate international expansion, particularly in European markets, and to build out enterprise sales capabilities. According to company statements, ARR had grown 4x year-over-year at the time of the round, with particular strength in financial services and e-commerce verticals.

The round also included strategic participation from several existing investors increasing their positions, reflecting confidence in the company's differentiated approach to streaming SQL and real-time analytics. Proceeds are being allocated toward expanding the engineering team by 50% over 18 months, with particular focus on query optimization and multi-cloud deployment capabilities.

---

## Series B Extension - August 2023
**Amount:** $15.2M  
**Pre-Money Valuation:** $385M  
**Post-Money Valuation:** $400M  
**Lead Investor:** Redpoint Ventures  
**Participants:** Kleiner Perkins, Lightspeed Venture Partners

A strategic extension round led by existing investor Redpoint Ventures provided additional runway and growth capital ahead of the company's Series C process. This extension was primarily driven by accelerated customer acquisition and the opportunity to capture market share during a period of increased enterprise adoption of real-time data architectures.

The funding enabled Materialize to expand its sales and customer success teams while maintaining its engineering hiring pace. The round was completed at a modest step-up valuation, with existing investors demonstrating continued support for the company's long-term vision and execution capabilities.

---

## Series B - September 2022
**Amount:** $48.5M  
**Pre-Money Valuation:** $335M  
**Post-Money Valuation:** $385M  
**Lead Investor:** Kleiner Perkins  
**Participants:** Lightspeed Venture Partners, Redpoint Ventures, Version One Ventures, Bessemer Venture Partners

Kleiner Perkins led Materialize's Series B, with partner Mamoon Hamid joining the board of directors. The round was raised during a period of strong customer growth, with the company reporting 250% year-over-year ARR expansion and notable enterprise wins including implementations at several Fortune 500 companies.

Funding was primarily allocated toward product development, specifically enhancing the platform's ability to handle larger-scale deployments and improving integration capabilities with existing data infrastructure tools. The round also supported the launch of Materialize Cloud, the company's fully-managed SaaS offering, which has since become the primary growth driver for new customer acquisition.

Notable in this round was the participation of several prominent angel investors from the data infrastructure ecosystem, including former executives from Snowflake, Databricks, and Confluent, providing valuable strategic guidance alongside capital.

---

## Series A - March 2021
**Amount:** $32.0M  
**Pre-Money Valuation:** $125M  
**Post-Money Valuation:** $157M  
**Lead Investor:** Lightspeed Venture Partners  
**Participants:** Redpoint Ventures, Version One Ventures, Individual Angels

Lightspeed Venture Partners led Materialize's Series A round, with partner Guru Chahal taking a board seat. The funding came as the company emerged from stealth mode with significant early traction among developer communities and initial enterprise pilot deployments.

The Series A enabled Materialize to transition from an open-source project to a commercial enterprise, funding the development of cloud-native capabilities and establishing go-to-market operations. Key initiatives included building out the founding team, establishing customer success operations, and developing enterprise security and compliance features.

The round was notable for its strong participation from Version One Ventures, reflecting confidence in the founding team's technical expertise and vision for simplifying real-time data processing workflows.

---

## Seed - June 2019
**Amount:** $11.5M  
**Pre-Money Valuation:** $35M  
**Post-Money Valuation:** $47M  
**Lead Investor:** Redpoint Ventures  
**Participants:** Version One Ventures, Several prominent angel investors

Redpoint Ventures led Materialize's seed round, recognizing the potential for the company's differential dataflow approach to address growing market demand for real-time analytics capabilities. The funding supported initial product development and the assembly of the founding engineering team.

Seed funding was primarily used for core platform development, establishing the foundational streaming SQL capabilities that would become Materialize's primary competitive differentiation. The round also supported early open-source community building efforts that have been crucial to the company's developer-first go-to-market strategy.
