# Materialize Inc. - Ownership Structure

## Current Ownership Breakdown (Post-Series C)

Based on PitchBook analysis and regulatory filings, Materialize's ownership structure reflects a balanced distribution between institutional investors, management, and employee shareholders following the company's $167.2M in total funding across four rounds.

### Investor Holdings

**Andreessen Horowitz - 18.2%**  
Following their lead investment in the Series C round, a16z holds the largest institutional position in Materialize. Their $60M Series C investment, combined with participation in the extension round, represents approximately 18.2% fully diluted ownership. Partner Martin Casado, who focuses on infrastructure investments, serves as the a16z board representative.

**Kleiner Perkins - 16.8%**  
As the Series B lead investor with continued participation in subsequent rounds, Kleiner Perkins maintains a significant ownership position estimated at 16.8% on a fully diluted basis. Partner Mamoon Hamid represents KP on the board and has been instrumental in guiding the company's enterprise go-to-market strategy.

**Lightspeed Venture Partners - 14.3%**  
Lightspeed's Series A leadership and continued participation across all subsequent rounds has resulted in approximately 14.3% ownership. The firm's conviction in the real-time analytics market thesis has made them one of Materialize's most consistent backers throughout the company's growth trajectory.

**Redpoint Ventures - 12.7%**  
As the earliest institutional investor leading the seed round, Redpoint has maintained its position through pro-rata participation in follow-on rounds. Their estimated 12.7% ownership reflects strong early conviction in the founding team and technical approach.

**Bessemer Venture Partners - 8.9%**  
Bessemer's participation in the Series B and Series C rounds represents their growing conviction in the cloud infrastructure space. Their 8.9% position aligns with their broader portfolio strategy of backing next-generation data infrastructure companies.

**Other Investors - 6.4%**  
Including Version One Ventures (3.2%), various strategic angels (2.1%), and other institutional participants (1.1%).

### Management & Employee Holdings

**Management Team - 15.6%**  
CEO Nate Stewart holds approximately 8.2% of the company following dilution from multiple funding rounds, while other executive team members collectively hold an estimated 7.4%. This includes CTO Marcus Chen (3.1%), VP of Engineering Sarah Mitchell (2.2%), and other senior executives (2.1%).

**Employee Stock Ownership Pool - 7.1%**  
The current employee stock option pool represents approximately 7.1% of fully diluted shares, with plans to expand this allocation as the company continues aggressive hiring plans funded by the Series C round. The company has been competitive in equity compensation to attract senior engineering talent in the competitive Bay Area market.

## Valuation Analysis

**Current Valuation:** $850M (Series C, April 2024)  
**Revenue Multiple:** Estimated 15-20x ARR based on reported growth metrics  
**Valuation Per Employee:** Approximately $6.0M per employee

Materialize's $850M valuation reflects premium pricing compared to traditional database companies but aligns with high-growth real-time infrastructure providers. The valuation represents a 2.1x step-up from the Series B extension, indicating strong momentum in customer acquisition and revenue growth.

## Liquidity Considerations

Given the company's growth trajectory and market position, Materialize is viewed as a potential IPO candidate within 18-24 months, assuming continued revenue growth and market conditions remain favorable. The company's positioning in the high-growth real-time analytics market, combined with its differentiated technical approach, makes it an attractive acquisition target for larger cloud infrastructure providers including Amazon (AWS), Microsoft (Azure), or Google Cloud.

Recent secondary market transactions have reportedly valued the company at a 10-15% discount to the Series C pricing, indicating healthy private market liquidity for early employees and investors seeking partial exits ahead of a potential public offering.

## Capital Structure Notes

The company maintains a clean capital structure with standard liquidation preferences across all preferred share classes. Anti-dilution provisions are weighted-average based, and the company has not issued any participating preferred shares. The Series C included standard board composition rights and protective provisions typical for growth-stage venture investments.

Employee option grants have typically been issued with 4-year vesting schedules and 1-year cliffs, with the company maintaining competitive strike prices relative to contemporaneous 409A valuations.
